02 — Seal

FAQ
Questions answered.
How do I know if K11 is right for my system?
Over 30 days to lose full charge? Micro-leak — K11 is made for this. 15–30 days? Medium leak, K11 may work. Under 15 days? Large leak requiring welding. The 2-in-1 formula seals and confirms results simultaneously with UV verification.
Is K11 safe for compressors, TXVs, and expansion valves?
Yes. K11 TapaFugas is polymer-free and only activates at leak points when it contacts air and moisture. Safe for all system components — TXVs, TEVs, capillary tubes, Schrader valves, and all common seal materials. ASHRAE 97 certified.
What refrigerants are compatible with K11?
All common refrigerants: R22, R410A, R32, R134a, R404A, R290, R600a, R1234yf, and all A2L replacements. The only exception is ammonia (R717). Compatible with all refrigerant oil types: mineral, POE, PAG, and alkylbenzene.
How much K11 do I need for my system?
10ml for systems up to 3TR (36,000 BTU). 20ml up to 5TR. 30ml up to 8TR. Over 8TR — mix with oil at 1:30 ratio (3.25%). Precision dosing prevents waste and ensures full coverage.
How long does the seal last?
The seal is permanent — it lasts the remaining operational life of the system. Documented cases holding strong after 5+ years of continuous operation. The product also circulates preventively to protect against future micro-leaks.
How do I order and how fast does Wylco ship?
Contact us for B2B pricing, wholesale orders, and distributor inquiries. We ship nationwide from Miami, FL with same-day dispatch on orders placed before 2 PM EST. Standard delivery is 2–5 business days.

Application Guide

How K11 works
in the field.

Step-by-step guide for HVAC-R technicians. From injection to UV verification — the complete K11 field process.

1

Classify the Leak

Determine if K11 is appropriate based on how quickly the system loses charge. Over 30 days = micro-leak (K11 recommended). 15–30 days = medium. Under 15 days = large (welding required).

2

Run the System

Operate the AC system normally before injection. The compressor must be running for correct distribution of K11 through the refrigerant circuit.

3

Connect the Adapter

Attach the K11 anti-backflow adapter to the low-pressure (suction) service port. Load the correct dose of K11 TapaFugas 2-in-1 into the syringe.

4

Inject K11

Slowly inject the full syringe into the circuit with the system running. The refrigerant flow carries K11 throughout the entire circuit within minutes.

5

Let K11 Circulate

Run the system for 15–30 minutes. K11 circulates through all components and activates permanently at any leak site it encounters.

6

Verify with UV Light

Use a UV or violet light to inspect the system. The fluorescent tracer in K11 marks the sealed point visibly. Document it. Show the client. Job complete.
